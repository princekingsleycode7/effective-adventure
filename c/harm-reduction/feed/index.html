]]> Standalone GCMS or LCMS screening for contamination of the sample.

The type of analysis depends o the type of sample. Our lab team will determine which method is best for your sample. In case you have any questions, please write us.

GCMS testing uses NIST 2014 library to detect tens of thousands of possibly harmful substances in the product. Detection is limited to volatile and semi-volatile compounds and the test cannot be used on peptide samples.

LCMS testing can detect contamination of peptide products with other peptides and degraded peptide. Detection is limited to peptides on our price list (you can ask our customer support for the full list).

Both tests give only identification of contaminants. Measurement of concentration can be offered for additional price once such substance is detected.

]]> CHNS elementary analysis is a gold standard in measuring the net peptide content. Instead of dissolution of the sample, it uses combustion. After the sample is burned down to basic elements, their amount is measured, which results in accurate net peptide content value. At the same time, the solubility issues which may happen with HPLC measurement of amount are solved for good with this method.

]]> Total Aerobic Microbial Count (TAMC) and Total Yeast and Mold Count (TYMC) are cultivation tests for microbial presence in the sample. In case the test fails, sample is not sterile.

Test requires unopened vials or ampoules, in order to prevent contamination of the sample during shipping and handling.

Sending less than recommended amount is possible, however, it may impact the results.

We do not recommend this test for oral samples, are orals do not need to be sterile for the safe use.
